What is Vascular Disease?

Vascular disease refers to any anomaly to the blood vessels (arteries and veins). The most common vascular diseases are peripheral artery disease, abdominal aortic aneurysm, carotid artery disease, arteriovenous malformation, critical limb-threatening ischemia, chronic venous insufficiency, deep vein thrombosis (DVT), and varicose veins.

Vascular diseases become more common with age, and patients with a family history of vascular and heart diseases are at higher risk.

Treatment Options Explore our available technologies

Non-Invasive Diagnostic Screening

Identifies arterial, blockages, venous disease, blood pressure cuffs are applied to the thighs, calves, and toes to identify diminished blood flow.

Atherectomy

A minimally invasive procedure designed to remove the build up of plaque in the arteries, thus returning blood flow to the area.

Angioplasty

A minimally invasive procedure performed to open up narrowed arteries or blocked vessels to improve blood flow.

Stent Placement

A stent may be placed in the artery in order to keep it open, which in turn reduces the chance of it narrowing again.

Radio-frequency Ablation

A minimally invasive procedure designed to reroute the blood supply from the problematic vein to a healthier vein.

Sclerotherapy

Involves injecting a solution into vein to remove varicose veins and reroute blood supply to a healthier vein.

Mesenteric artery disease

Mesenteric vascular disease is a condition that develops when the arteries in the abdomen that supply the intestines with blood become narrowed due to the build-up of plaque (a process called atherosclerosis). The result is a lack of blood supply to the intestines.

Treating aneurysms

Endovascular Surgery can treat Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm.

AV access placement

The surgeon usually places an AV fistula in the forearm or upper arm. An AV fistula causes extra pressure and extra blood to flow into the vein, making it grow large and strong. The larger vein provides easy, reliable access to blood vessels.

A quick self-check?

Do you have any of these existing conditions?

Diabetes, High Cholesterol, Hypertension, History of Smoking, History of Kidney Diseases, History of Heart Diseases


Are you experiencing any of the symptoms?

Discoloration on toes

Blue or bulging/swollen veins

Foot ulcers or wounds on the leg

Leg heaviness or soreness

Lower or Upper limbs cramping

One foot colder than the other

Discomfort in limbs that affect daily routine
